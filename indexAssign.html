My nickname is Dora and I'm from the baby boomer generation. I'm retired but hope to be working part-time in the future. I have an active lifestyle that includes hiking, running, biking and ice skating. I played ice hockey for about 15 years as an adult. I'm a big fan of professional and college hockey (Go Avs, Go PWHL, Go Pios!) I love being in the mountains; when I'm in my favorite mountain areas, I feel my best and experience a lot of joy and happiness.

About 20 years ago, a few good friends of mine started playing adult, recreational ice hockey. I watched some games and caught the bug. After the 1998 Olympics in Nagano, girl's and women's hockey grew exponentially in the U.S. A fledgling women's league was formed in my area that focused on women of all ages who wanted to learn to skate and play the game. I joined up. After a year of learning and practicing skills, the league began real games. I never looked back! Since then I played in several leagues for about 15 years (both women's and coed leagues). My teammates and I played in tournaments around the state, around the country, in Canada and in Iceland! I even coached little kids for a few years. Ice hockey changed my life. Lifelong friendships were formed. It was the greatest of times!

In the past several years I experienced some tough life circumstances. My health has not been the best. I'm currently trying to get back to a good state of health and am working hard at improving my fitness level. I want to (and need to) work part-time, hopefully remotely at a job that I truly enjoy. Other plans include playing ice hockey again and improving my running and endurance activities. Goals include participating in running races from 5K to marathon distance.
